| Georgian | Transliteration | Role | Voice |
|----------|-----------------|------|-------|

| **მკრიმანი** | **Mkrimani** | Gilgamesh-as-God (inner voice) | Countertenor |
| **მთავარი** | **Mtavari** | Gilgamesh-as-Man (inner voice) | Tenor |
| **ბანი** | **Bani** | Gilgamesh-as-Shadow (inner voice) | Bass |
| **გუნდა** | **Gunda** | Chorus — People of Uruk | SATB |

**Georgian instruments:**
- **ლამური** (*Lamuri*) — Svan bone flute (offstage, represents Enkidu's nature)
- **ჩუნირი** (*Chuniri*) — Svan two-string fiddle (represents Ninsun/memory)
- **პანდური** (*Panduri*) — three-string Kakhetian lute

> **NOTE ON THE TRIO:** Mkrimani, Mtavari, and Bani are invisible to all other characters — they are the three aspects of Gilgamesh's soul. Only Ninsun senses them. Their musical arc: T1 *ppp* (Act I) → T17 *ff* (Act IV) → T2 *fff* atonal (Act V) → T3 *ff* (Finale).

# ACT I — TYRANNY AND SOLITUDE
## მოქმედება I — ტირანია და მარტოობა
## *Moqmedeba I — T'irania da Mart'ooba*

**Scenes 1–3 · ~35 minutes**
**Tonal center:** G minor → D minor
**Folk songs:** Tsintskaro (T1), Beri-Berikoba (T16), Virishkhau (T6)

**Color palette:** Terracotta · Sand · Night blue
**Setting:** The streets of Uruk. Harsh sunlight. Gilgamesh's throne at center.
The Trio is always in shadow, invisible to the crowd.

## SCENE 1 — URUK IN MOURNING
### სცენა 1 — ურუქი გოდებაში / *Sk'ena 1 — Uruki Godebashi*
**Location:** Streets of Uruk. Dawn.
**Key:** G minor · **Time:** 5/8 · **Tempo:** ♩.=58, *Allegro giocoso*
**Theme T16** — *Beri-Berikoba* (Gurian rhythmic song) + **T1** *Tsintskaro* (ppp)

---

### №1 — CHORUS: "Uruk Mourns"
*[Gurian Beri-Berikoba, 5/8, ff]*

**Georgian:**
```
ურუქი გლოვობს, მეფე არ გვესმის!
დღე და ღამე ვშრომობთ, ის კი სძინავს!
ჩვენი ძალა მას სჭირდება,
ჩვენი სისხლი მას სწყურია!
```

**Transliteration:**
```
Uruki glovobs, mepe ar gvesmis!
Dghe da ghame vshromorbt, is ki spinavs!
Chveni dzala mas schirdeba,
Chveni siskhli mas sts'q'uria!
```

**English:**
```
Uruk mourns — the king does not hear us!
Day and night we labor, but he sleeps!
He needs our strength,
He thirsts for our blood!
```
